COLOR RULES

- Neutrals (black, white, gray, navy, beige) anchor bold colors.
- Monochrome outfits look sleek; vary texture to keep them interesting.
- Complementary colors (blue/orange, red/green) pop; use one as an accent.
- Analogous colors (blue/teal/green) feel calm and cohesive.
- Warm colors advance; cool colors recede. Use this to balance proportions.
- Tone: light tones feel fresh and airy; dark tones feel formal and slimming.
- Saturation: high-saturation colors feel bold; muted colors feel refined and easy to mix.
- Contrast: high contrast reads sharp and energetic; low contrast reads soft and subtle.
- Undertone: match warm skin undertones with warm colors (peach, olive, mustard); cool undertones with cool colors (berry, icy blue).
- Value balance: pair one dark, one mid, one light for a balanced outfit.
- Color temperature mix: if you mix warm and cool, keep one dominant and use the other as accents.
- Pattern color control: pull a single color from the pattern to tie the rest of the outfit together.
